Homeopathy is a therapeutic approach that applies the “law of similars” to encourage the healing processes within the organism. The key principles of homeopathy include:

1. Law of similars (simile similibus curentur, i.e., let likes be cured by likes).


2. Drug proving - Medicinal substances are tested on healthy individuals to determine their ability to induce specific diseases or symptoms.

3. Drug dynamization - Medicines used for treatment must be “spirit-like” to resonate with the organism’s vital energy, which is also “spirit-like”.

4. Single remedy - Homeopathy addresses the overall state of health, as reflected by signs and symptoms over a given period, recognizing that no two states can coexist simultaneously within an organism.

5. Minimal dose - The ideal dose is the smallest amount sufficient to trigger the healing response.

6. Treating the totality - Homeopathy considers the organism as a whole rather than focusing on isolated parts.
